# Learning Record 0002: Question Generation in Unfamiliar Domains

## Date
2026-06-14

## Context
The learner asked for a mental-model lesson on how to build reviewer judgment in unfamiliar areas and how to know what questions to ask.

## Key Insights
- The goal is not immediate expertise; it is disciplined question generation.
- Strong questions ask for commitments, trust relationships, assumptions, attacker capability, and evidence.
- The same question families transfer across web, mobile, OAuth, cloud, and AI systems.
- Feeling lost usually means the reviewer has not yet anchored on the asset, the trust boundary, or the evidence requirement.

## Working Rules
- Ask what the system must guarantee, not just what features it has.
- Reuse five question families: asset, boundary, assumption, capability, evidence.
- Escalate questions from orientation to proof.
- Rewrite vague technology questions into attacker- and trust-focused questions.

## Implications For Future Lessons
- Practice with side-by-side domain translations is valuable.
- Domain-specific follow-up lessons should focus on how the same mental moves surface different questions.

## Open Questions
- How to calibrate the number of questions asked during a time-boxed review.
- How to use AI to generate candidate questions without over-trusting its coverage.
