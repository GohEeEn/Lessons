# Learning Record 0004: Risk Evaluation and Prioritization in Context

## Date
2026-06-14

## Context
The learner shifted focus from design-phase threat modeling mechanics to determining and evaluating security risk and security impact for prioritization decisions.

## Key Insights
- Risk prioritization must be context-driven, not severity-label-driven.
- A transparent model combining likelihood, impact, and confidence is practical for design reviews.
- Impact should be written as a concrete business scenario, not only a numeric label.
- High uncertainty near critical assets should increase review urgency, not reduce it.

## Working Rules
- Score risks with explicit rationale.
- Tie every top-priority risk to a testable security requirement and evidence plan.
- Include confidence and unknowns in prioritization decisions.
- Require owners and due windows for P0/P1 risks.

## Implications For Future Lessons
- Next practical exercise should score real project risks and defend prioritization choices in review language.
- Future coaching should include risk acceptance criteria and exception handling.
