Lesson 5: Security Risk Evaluation and Prioritization

In this lesson: you will learn how to determine risk from context, evaluate impact credibly, and prioritize security work without relying on guesswork or severity labels alone.

Core shift: risk is not the same as vulnerability severity. Risk is the combination of exploitability, business impact, and context confidence. Prioritization should follow risk-to-business, not loudest finding.

1. Inputs Required to Evaluate Security Risk

Input What to capture Why it affects prioritization
Asset value Data sensitivity, critical function, legal/regulatory exposure Higher-value assets amplify impact
Trust boundary context Where untrusted actors influence system behavior Boundary exposure increases exploit paths
Attacker capability Prerequisites, skill level, access needed, chain complexity Determines practical likelihood
Control posture Existing preventive, detective, and reactive controls Reduces likelihood and/or blast radius
Business impact scenario Concrete failure story: financial, operational, legal, trust damage Avoids abstract “high/medium/low” inflation
Confidence level Evidence quality and unknowns Low confidence should raise review urgency

2. A Practical Scoring Model for Prioritization

Use a transparent model in design reviews. Keep it simple enough to defend in a meeting.

Recommended model:
Risk Priority = Likelihood x Impact x Confidence Modifier
Likelihood (1-5)
  • 1: Highly impractical
  • 3: Feasible with meaningful effort
  • 5: Straightforward and repeatable
Impact (1-5)
  • 1: Minor localized effect
  • 3: Material impact to one service or team
  • 5: Business-critical, regulatory, or systemic impact
Confidence Modifier
  • 1.0: Strong evidence
  • 1.2: Some unknowns
  • 1.4: Major uncertainty or missing evidence

Why confidence can increase priority: unknown risk near critical assets is usually under-estimated. If uncertainty is high, prioritize discovery and verification.

3. Evaluate Impact as a Scenario, Not a Label

Instead of “Impact: High,” write a one-paragraph impact story.

Impact dimension Questions to ask
Confidentiality What sensitive data can be exposed? At what scale?
Integrity Can attacker alter high-trust decisions, records, or transactions?
Availability What service outage or degradation occurs, and for whom?
Safety or legal Does it create compliance, legal, contractual, or physical harm?
Reputation or trust Would this incident materially reduce customer or partner trust?

4. Prioritization Output You Should Produce

At the end of a review, deliver a ranked risk list with clear decisions.

Risk ID Score and rationale Priority tier Decision
R-001 Likelihood 4, Impact 5, Confidence 1.2 P0 Block release until control and test evidence exist
R-002 Likelihood 3, Impact 4, Confidence 1.0 P1 Fix in current release, verify with integration tests
R-003 Likelihood 2, Impact 3, Confidence 1.4 P1-discovery Time-box investigation to reduce uncertainty
Common mistake: prioritizing solely by technical severity (for example CVSS) without business context, exploit preconditions, or control coverage. Severity is an input, not the decision.

5. How to Decide Priority Tiers

  • P0: likely and business-critical. Must be addressed before launch or before enabling exposed pathways.
  • P1: significant but not immediate catastrophic risk. Planned in current cycle with explicit owner.
  • P2: meaningful hardening item. Scheduled backlog with clear dependency and due window.
  • P3: low-risk or low-confidence issue requiring monitoring or deferred investigation.

6. Design-Phase Prioritization Pattern

  1. Rank by context-based risk score.
  2. Map each top risk to a testable security requirement.
  3. Attach implementation and runtime evidence expectations.
  4. Define release gates for P0 and selected P1 risks.
  5. Create discovery tasks for high-uncertainty risks.

7. Worked Example (OAuth Browser Client)

Risk statement Evaluation Priority decision
Stolen refresh token enables persistent session access Likelihood 4 (browser-side exposure path exists), Impact 5 (persistent account access), Confidence 1.2 (storage details partially unknown) P0: require rotation, reuse detection, and revocation evidence before release
Revocation propagation delay allows post-revoke token use Likelihood 3, Impact 4, Confidence 1.4 (latency SLO unproven) P1-discovery: define SLO, instrument latency, validate failure behavior
