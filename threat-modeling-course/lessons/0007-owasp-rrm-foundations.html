Lesson 7: OWASP Risk Rating Methodology Foundations

Goal: understand OWASP Risk Rating Methodology (RRM) from scratch, then apply it to your existing design-phase prioritization model.

Primary source: OWASP Risk Rating Methodology

1) What OWASP RRM Is

OWASP RRM is a structured way to rate risk severity by combining:

  1. Likelihood: how feasible exploitation is.
  2. Impact: how much technical and business damage exploitation causes.

At a high level: Risk = Likelihood x Impact.

It is not a single universal truth model. OWASP explicitly expects teams to customize factor definitions and weighting to fit their organization.

2) The RRM Flow (Simplified)

  1. Identify a risk scenario.
  2. Estimate likelihood factors.
  3. Estimate impact factors.
  4. Combine them to determine severity.
  5. Prioritize what to fix first.
  6. Customize model for your context.

3) Likelihood Factors in RRM

GroupExamplesQuestion it answers
Threat agent factors Skill level, motive, opportunity, attacker population size How capable and motivated is the attacker set?
Vulnerability factors Ease of discovery, ease of exploit, awareness, detection likelihood How easy is this path to find and use?

4) Impact Factors in RRM

GroupExamplesQuestion it answers
Technical impact Loss of confidentiality, integrity, availability, accountability What happens to systems and data?
Business impact Financial damage, reputation damage, non-compliance, privacy violation What happens to business outcomes?
Important: for prioritization decisions, business impact should dominate technical impact when business context is known.

5) Where RRM Fits in Your Current Framework

Your current framework is already deterministic:

Priority Score = Likelihood (1-5) x Impact (1-5) x Confidence Modifier

Use RRM to enrich how you compute Likelihood and Impact, then map into your 1-5 scale.

RRM outputYour framework fieldWhy
RRM likelihood estimateLikelihood (1-5)Keeps deterministic scoring while preserving richer reasoning.
RRM impact estimateImpact (1-5)Preserves business consequence focus.
Evidence quality (not in core RRM formula)Confidence ModifierHandles design-phase uncertainty explicitly.

6) Minimal Worked Example

Scenario: stolen refresh token replay in browser OAuth flow.
  1. RRM likelihood factors suggest high feasibility (token persistence + weak replay detection).
  2. RRM business impact suggests high damage (account takeover, potential regulatory exposure).
  3. Map to your scale: Likelihood 4, Impact 5.
  4. Confidence 1.2 due to incomplete revocation latency evidence.
  5. Priority score: 4 x 5 x 1.2 = 24, so P0.

7) Challenge This Method

  • More factors can create false precision if not calibrated.
  • Design-phase estimates can be unstable with missing implementation details.
  • Different reviewers can score differently without periodic calibration sessions.

Mitigation:

  1. Keep score anchors explicit.
  2. Limit decimal precision.
  3. Require short narrative rationale per score.
  4. Run periodic calibration workshops.

8) What to Practice Next

  1. Take one risk from your current design.
  2. Score it once with your base model only.
  3. Score it again using RRM-enriched factor thinking.
  4. Compare decision quality and confidence.
