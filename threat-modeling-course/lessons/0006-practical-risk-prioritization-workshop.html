Lesson 6: Practical Risk Prioritization Workshop

Objective: apply Lesson 5 to realistic design-phase threats and make defensible priority decisions.

Use this formula from Lesson 5: Priority Score = Likelihood x Impact x Confidence Modifier.
Suggested confidence modifiers: 1.0 strong evidence, 1.2 some unknowns, 1.4 major uncertainty.

How to use this workshop

  1. Read each scenario.
  2. Score likelihood and impact.
  3. Choose confidence modifier based on evidence quality.
  4. Decide final priority (P0/P1/P2/P3).
  5. Write one sentence defending your choice.
Do not optimize for the exact number. Optimize for consistency and quality of reasoning.

Calibration Debrief

Question What good answers include
Why did you pick that likelihood? Exploit preconditions, attacker skill, boundary exposure, and chain complexity.
Why did you pick that impact? Concrete business scenario: financial, regulatory, operational, trust consequences.
Why that confidence modifier? Evidence quality and unknowns, not personal certainty.
Why final priority? Score plus escalation rules and release consequences.
If two reviewers produce very different priorities for the same scenario, do not average blindly. Resolve disagreement by comparing assumptions and evidence quality.
Completion criteria:
  • You can score all 3 scenarios and explain decisions clearly.
  • You can justify at least one escalation override.
  • You can separate "low severity" from "low business risk" when context changes.
