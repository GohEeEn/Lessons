Risk Prioritization Rubric

Use this for design reviews and early threat modeling. Keep scoring transparent and defensible.

1) Risk Formula

Priority Score = Likelihood (1-5) x Impact (1-5) x Confidence Modifier (1.0 / 1.2 / 1.4)

2) Likelihood Scale

ScoreDescriptionTypical indicators
1ImprobableHigh prerequisites, rare attacker capability, no clear chain
2LowComplex exploitation with strong controls in place
3FeasibleRealistic chain with moderate effort
4LikelyKnown technique with weak/missing controls
5Highly likelyEasy, repeatable, exposed path

3) Impact Scale

ScoreDescriptionTypical indicators
1MinorSmall localized effect, no sensitive data or material outage
2LowLimited user impact, recoverable quickly
3ModerateService/team-level impact or meaningful data issue
4HighMulti-team or external customer impact, contractual exposure
5CriticalBusiness-critical interruption, major breach, legal/regulatory consequences

4) Confidence Modifier

ModifierUse whenAction
1.0Evidence strong, assumptions validatedProceed with normal prioritization
1.2Some unknowns or partial evidenceAdd explicit validation tasks
1.4High uncertainty, missing key dataEscalate discovery and avoid deferral without owner

5) Priority Bands

Score rangePriorityExpected decision
20+P0Block release or exposure until mitigated and verified
12-19.9P1Fix this cycle with assigned owner and evidence plan
6-11.9P2Planned backlog with timeline and dependency tracking
<6P3Monitor, defer with rationale, or close as accepted risk

6) Mandatory Output Fields Per Risk

FieldRequired content
Risk statementAttacker capability + boundary + asset impact
Score rationaleWhy likelihood and impact were assigned
Control decisionPreventive/detective/reactive plan
Verification planDesign, implementation, and runtime evidence expected
Owner and due dateNamed accountable person/team

7) Review Sanity Checks

  • Do not prioritize by CVSS only.
  • Do not ignore high-uncertainty risks near critical assets.
  • Do not allow mitigation without evidence requirements.
  • Do not close a risk without explicit acceptance rationale.
