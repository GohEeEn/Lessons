The Eight Domains

The CISSP Common Body of Knowledge (CBK) is organised into eight domains. They are not a flat list — they form a layered system. Governance sets the rules; engineering builds the controls; identity manages who is trusted; assurance validates that everything works; and software security ensures what we build doesn't undo all of the above.

The One Mindset Shift That Changes Everything

CISSP is not a technical certification. It tests whether you can think like a senior security manager — someone who reasons about risk, business impact, and priorities, not someone who configures tools.

⚠️ The most common CISSP exam mistake — choosing the most technically correct answer instead of the most managerially appropriate one.

When two answers look correct, apply this tiebreaker in order:
  1. Protect people before systems or data.
  2. Prevent before detect, detect before respond.
  3. Choose the action that addresses risk based on priority — not just severity.
  4. When in doubt: the action that requires least disruption for highest risk reduction wins.
