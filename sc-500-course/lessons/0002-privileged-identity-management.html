Privileged Identity Management

Just-in-Time Access, Eligible Assignments & Access Reviews

📚 Track 1: Identity, Access & Governance ⏱ ~45 min 🗓 Lesson 0002
Lesson goal: Understand how PIM eliminates standing privilege — the difference between eligible and active assignments, how the activation workflow operates, what settings control it, and how access reviews keep entitlements clean over time.

1. The Standing Privilege Problem

Before PIM, privileged roles were assigned permanently — a user given Global Administrator held it 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, whether or not they were actively doing admin work. This creates a disproportionate blast radius:

  • A phished Global Admin credential grants an attacker full tenant control, immediately, at any hour.
  • Admins accumulate roles over time and rarely give them back — role sprawl grows silently.
  • There is no audit trail of when someone used their privilege, only that they had it.
Threat modelling link: In STRIDE terms, standing privilege is an Elevation of Privilege pre-condition gift to an attacker. PIM removes it by making privilege time-bound and request-justified — the attacker's stolen credential becomes nearly useless outside an active window.

Microsoft Entra PIM (Privileged Identity Management) enforces just-in-time (JIT) access: a principal is eligible for a role but must explicitly activate it, proving identity and providing justification, for a limited time window.

2. Eligible vs Active Assignments

PIM introduces a distinction the standard RBAC model does not have:

3. The Activation Workflow

When a user needs to use an eligible role, they trigger the PIM activation flow. The exact steps depend on what the role administrator has configured, but the canonical sequence is:

sequenceDiagram actor User participant PIM as Microsoft Entra PIM participant Approver participant EntraID as Entra ID User->>PIM: Request activation (role + duration + justification) PIM->>User: Prompt: complete MFA User->>PIM: MFA satisfied PIM->>Approver: Send approval request (email + portal notification) Approver->>PIM: Approve request PIM->>EntraID: Grant role assignment (time-limited) EntraID-->>User: Role active — access granted Note over EntraID: Timer runs (max 8 h in this example) EntraID->>PIM: Activation window expires PIM->>EntraID: Revoke role assignment automatically EntraID-->>User: Role removed — back to Eligible

If approval is not required for a role, PIM grants the role immediately after MFA. If the user does not activate before the end of their eligible assignment period, the eligible assignment itself may also expire and require renewal.

4. PIM Role Settings

Each role in PIM has its own settings page. Role administrators (typically Privileged Role Administrator or Global Administrator) control these per-role:

SettingWhat it controlsRecommended for high-privilege roles
Maximum activation duration How long a single activation can last (1 min – 24 h) 8 h or less for Global Admin / Owner
Require MFA on activation Forces an MFA challenge before the role activates Always enable for privileged roles
Require justification User must enter a business reason before activation Enable — creates an audit trail per activation
Require approval A designated approver must approve before the role activates Enable for Global Admin, Security Admin, Privileged Role Admin
Require ticket information User must provide a change-control or ITSM ticket number Enable where a change management process exists
Notify on activation Email alert to the role administrator when someone activates Always enable — real-time visibility of privilege use
Exam scenario pattern: You will often see a question phrased as: "Ensure no one holds Global Administrator permanently, activations require a reason and a second approver, and the security team is alerted." The answer is: set maximum activation duration, enable justification, enable approval (with approver), enable notifications — all in PIM role settings.

6. Access Reviews

JIT solves the duration problem. Access reviews solve the accumulation problem: even eligible assignments pile up if no one periodically asks "does this person still need this role?"

An access review in PIM is a scheduled recertification campaign:

  1. A review is created for a role (e.g., "recertify all Global Admin eligible assignments every 90 days").
  2. Reviewers are notified — this can be self-review, manager review, or a designated reviewer.
  3. Reviewers approve (keep) or deny (remove) each assignment.
  4. When the review period closes, PIM auto-applies the decision — including auto-removing assignments where no response was given (configurable).
Why it matters: Access reviews are the mechanism that prevents JIT from becoming a graveyard of forgotten eligible assignments. The exam will test your understanding of who can create reviews, what happens to non-responders, and what the auto-apply action is.
Review settingOptions

7. PIM Audit Logs & Alerts

PIM records every event — assignment changes, activations, approvals, denials — in the Entra ID audit log. Key operational points:

  • Audit history: filter by role, user, or date range to see exactly who activated what and when, with their justification text.
  • Built-in alerts: PIM ships pre-configured alerts for high-risk patterns — e.g., "Roles are being activated too frequently", "Too many Global Administrators", "Roles are assigned outside of PIM".
  • Roles assigned outside PIM: if someone bypasses PIM and assigns a privileged role directly (via standard RBAC), PIM raises an alert. This is a critical signal of misconfiguration or shadow IT.
Exam trap: PIM alerts on "Roles are assigned outside of PIM" do not automatically block the assignment — they surface it for remediation. To enforce that PIM is the only path to privileged roles, you use Azure Policy to deny direct privileged role assignments.
